Abstract

Traditionally, the art world has been dependent on physical galleries and face-to-face auctions, which are expensive, inaccessible and limited to certain places. Lots of art lovers, students, and emerging artists do not have the opportunity to access such a platform because of geographical and economic reasons. The aim of this project is to create a web-based artwork auction system combining both auction services and the interactive interface that would facilitate the accessibility of art trading and appreciation. The system will enable registered users to view works of art, register on timed auctions and monitor their bidding progress in real time. In addition, the platform will provide supporting features such as user registration, bidding and selling arts, and a simple notification system. From the computing perspective, this project combines concepts from database management, web technologies, system analysis and design, and UX design. Its application fields are e-commerce simulation, digital marketplace, and online community platforms. The users targeted are artists or sellers (where one uploads the artwork to be auctioned), buyers or collectors (where one browses and places a bid). The final product will provide a platform that allows users access to art auctions, builds an online art community, and creates an engaging, interactive, and accessible online environment.

Project Objectives

  1. To study existing art auction platforms and digital marketplaces and identify key features and limitations.
  2. To design and develop an online art auction website with the necessary modules such as user registration, artwork upload, auction creation, and bidding system.
  3. To test and evaluate the proposed system in terms of functionality and usability to ensure its effectiveness.

System Functions

1. User Registration

Users can create an account with their username, email address, and password. Registered users can log in to their account and take actions like creating auctions or placing bids.

2. Upload Artwork & Create Auction

Users can upload artwork by providing details such as artwork title, description, image, and starting bid price. The system will generate an auction record.

3. Auction Browsing & Search

Users can view available artworks to auction. The system provides basic search and filter options (e.g., category, or price range).

4. Bidding System

Users may make bids on auctions that are currently ongoing. The system automatically changes the existing highest bid and reminds users when outbid.

5. Auction Results & History

Users can view the results of completed auctions, including the winning bid and bidder details. They can also access their personal bidding history.

6. Notifications & Alerts

The system notifies users of important updates, such as being outbid, winning an auction, or when a new auction is created.

System Requirements

Functional Requirements

  1. User Registration – Users create accounts using email and password, with data stored securely in MySQL.
  2. Art Upload and Auction Creation – Registered sellers upload artwork with details such as title, description, starting bid, and auction duration.
  3. Auction Browsing and Search – Buyers browse auctions and filter by category, price range, or artist.
  4. Bidding System – Users place real-time bids, with automatic updates of the current highest bid.
  5. Auction Results and History – Users can review past auctions, winning bids, and personal bidding history.
  6. Notifications and Alerts – Alerts are sent when a user is outbid, wins an auction, or when a new auction begins.

Non-Functional Requirements

  1. Performance – The system must handle concurrent bidding with real-time updates.
  2. Security – Use HTTPS to protect website and user data.
  3. Scalability – The architecture must support an increasing number of users and auctions.
  4. Usability – Interfaces must be intuitive and responsive across devices.
  5. Maintainability – The codebase should follow modular design for easy updates and debugging.
